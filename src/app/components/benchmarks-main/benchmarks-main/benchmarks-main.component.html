CFD Benchmarks

CFD benchmarks serve as critical tools for validating numerical models and algorithms. They provide a standardized platform for assessing the strengths and weaknesses of different CFD approaches. The use of benchmarks ensures that CFD simulations are not only computationally efficient but also physically realistic, enhancing confidence in the predictive capabilities of the employed numerical methods. In essence, CFD benchmarks, such as the flow around a cylinder and the rising bubble, facilitate the advancement of CFD methodologies by establishing a common ground for assessment. These benchmarks contribute to the ongoing refinement of numerical techniques, promoting the application of CFD to real-world engineering challenges with greater confidence and accuracy.
