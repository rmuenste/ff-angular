Particle Sedimentation

Particle sedimentation is a fundamental benchmark for the evaluation of numerical algorithms for fluid-structure interaction problems. This benchmark involves the simulation of a solid particle settling under gravity in a viscous fluid, providing insights into the dynamics of particle motion and fluid flow interaction. The benchmark tests various aspects of numerical methods including mesh handling, coupling strategies, and accuracy of force calculations.
The benchmark problems are studied with different computational fluid dynamics software packages to evaluate their capability in handling moving boundaries and fluid-particle interactions. Key observables include particle velocity, position, and the forces acting on the particle during the sedimentation process.
This benchmark addresses several important questions in computational fluid dynamics:
  • How accurately can different solvers handle fluid-structure interaction problems?
  • What is the optimal mesh strategy for moving boundary problems?
  • How do different time stepping schemes affect the stability and accuracy of the solution?

Verification and Results

The particle sedimentation benchmark has been studied with various computational fluid dynamics codes to evaluate their accuracy and efficiency in handling fluid-structure interaction problems. The benchmark focuses on both qualitative and quantitative validation of the numerical methods.

Key results include the time evolution of particle velocity, which should asymptotically approach the theoretical terminal velocity. The particle position as a function of time provides insights into the settling dynamics and can be compared with analytical solutions for the limiting cases.
